Frequently Asked Questions

Why?

Plastic bags that end up in bodies of water are harmful to aquatic life and birds. Once in the water, they often go un-noticed. Plastic bags caught in trees serve as an indicator of the level of plastic bag pollution in an area. Through BagsInTrees.org, we can highlight plastic bag pollution throughout the world and provide a more convincing argument for reducing or eliminating the use of plastic bags.

My Instagram didn't show up on the map where I took it. What happened?

When you take a photo with the Instagram app, it records your location when you submit the photo (after you have applied all filters and added your message and tag). If you took the photo with the Instagram app and continued walking, it will show up a few blocks down the street where you actually submitted the photo.

Thankfully, your smartphone made a copy of the image and recorded its true location. If this happens, simply delete the photo from Instagram and add it again, this time selecting it from your phone's photos.

I tagged my photo but it's not showing on the bag map.

There could be a few reasons for this.
First, make sure you have allowed Instagram to access your location and that on your mobile device, you enable your location services for your camera.
It's possible that Bags In Trees hasn't yet picked up the most recent images. Give it a few minutes and refresh the map.
Finally, take a look around the area you took the photo and see if you find it nearby. It's possible that your phone didn't know your location 100% accurately. Tall buildings can often cause your GPS location to be incorrect.
